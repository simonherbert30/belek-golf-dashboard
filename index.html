Pasha — strategic notes
  • Wide, forgiving fairways — most beginner-friendly
  • 57 cleverly placed bunkers (most of the 3)
  • Tricky dog-legs on several holes
  • Undulating greens — approach accuracy key
  • 6 lakes but mostly avoidable
  • Best for: warming up, scoring well
Kaya — strategic notes
  • Narrowest fairways at ~250 m landing zone
  • Water on 9+ holes — most penal of the 3
  • Risk/reward on holes 3, 5, 13, 17, 18
  • Bermuda greens — fast, true
  • 4 bridges over water
  • Best for: decision making off the tee
Carya — strategic notes
  • Tightest from yellow — back tees are wider
  • Heather GUR — free drop if ball in heather
  • Very fast, sloping bent grass greens
  • Deep bunkers — hard to escape
  • Europe's 1st fully lit 18-hole course
  • Best for: experience, the ultimate challenge
Carya Day→Night tip: Your 15:27 tee time means you start in full daylight and transition to floodlights around holes 6–8. Lights are mounted 36 m high at 250 Lux — genuinely playable. The tricky window is the twilight transition: aim for fairway centre, avoid aggressive lines on holes 6–9. Ball flight becomes harder to track beyond 180 m in the early lit phase. The course turns into a completely different experience once fully lit — enjoy it.
